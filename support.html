Managing your subscription

RedlineRoom Unlimited removes the free tier’s transcription cap. Subscriptions are billed by Apple through your Apple Account and renew automatically until cancelled.

To manage, cancel, or turn off auto-renew:

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPad or iPhone.
  2. Tap your name at the top → Subscriptions (or Media & Purchases → Subscriptions).
  3. Tap RedlineRoom and choose a plan change or Cancel Subscription.

Restore a purchase

If you reinstall the app or switch devices, tap Restore Purchases on the paywall or in Settings inside RedlineRoom to re-activate an existing subscription. Restores use the Apple Account that made the original purchase.

Refunds

All purchases and refunds are handled by Apple. Request a refund at reportaproblem.apple.com.

Frequently asked

Is there a free version?

Yes. Drawing, the running punch list, and PDF/CSV export are always free with no limits. The free tier also includes 20 AI transcriptions every 30 days. You can always type an item by hand for free, even after reaching the cap. Unlimited only lifts the transcription cap.

What does Unlimited cost?

  • Monthly — $12.99 / month
  • Annual — $99.00 / year, with a 7-day free trial for new subscribers

Does it work offline?

Markup, the punch list, and PDF/CSV export work fully offline. Transcriptions captured while offline queue up and complete automatically when you reconnect.

What happens to my drawings and photos?

Only a single cropped image of each markup you choose to transcribe is sent for handwriting-to-text, and it is not stored on our servers. We never send your full drawing, project names, or personal data, and there is no account. Photos you attach to an item stay on your device and appear only in exports you choose to share. See our Privacy Policy for the full detail.

The transcription looks wrong — what do I do?

RedlineRoom shows its confidence and flags anything unclear for you to review. You confirm or edit every item before it’s counted or exported, so nothing is auto-accepted. If you see consistent misreads on a certain handwriting style, email us a description — it helps us improve.
